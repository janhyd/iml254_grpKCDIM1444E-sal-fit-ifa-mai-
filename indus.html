What is Industrial Home Design?


Industrial interiors are defined by the architectural elements within a space.
Industrial interior design is raw, almost unfinished, providing a casual atmosphere that's relaxing to live in. By keeping the space practical and uncluttered, this style is welcoming and laidback.

Some of the characteristics of Industrial Interior Design:



Color Palette:

Colors are usually more neutral and characteristic of the building materials used. Think grays, whites, browns and blacks. Elements like brick, wood, and steal heavily influence the color scheme.

Main colors should stay neutral, almost monochromatic. Gray tones that match concrete, white, or black are all perfect for this style.
Accent colors are flexible, though typically used sparingly. Add pops of vivid color for a fresh look, or stick to rust, wood, or brick tones to keep the clean industrial aesthetic.
Iron, steel, glass, chrome, copper, or brass finishes all work incredibly well in this aesthetic.



Décor:

Embrace open areas and negative space, this allows the décor and furniture to speak for themselves. Adding a textured rug, a vintage coffee table, or a piece of abstract instantly enhances the personality within a room.

Incorporating some plants is a good way to freshen up the space while adding some color.
Include furniture that is made from wood, iron, or steel. It adds to the space while also being functional.
Think coffee tables with casters, old metal lockers, or wire baskets and shelving.
Framed vintage photographs or abstract paintings work well in this space.
For lighting, check out floor lamps, pendants, or track lights.
